## Themes

**Agent-era infrastructure:** Every layer of the internet was built for human users. Payments, identity, discovery, compute---they all assume a person at the keyboard. When software agents act on their own, those layers break. I'm mapping the five that have to be rebuilt: rails, protocols, discovery, identity, and payments. The [agent-era infrastructure map](https://lab.sideband.pub/map/) tracks what's emerging.

**Agent payments:** How do agents pay for things when the transaction costs more than the service? The current payment stack can't handle sub-cent transactions across dozens of services per session. I wrote about the split between Visa/Mastercard and permissionless protocols like Lightning and Cashu in [agent payments have a three-body problem](https://sideband.pub/p/agent-payments-have-a-three-body).
